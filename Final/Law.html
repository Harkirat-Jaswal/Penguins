Law

The ancient Anatolian civilization's advanced law


The Hittites had a well-developed legal system, which is known from a collection of laws and legal texts that have been preserved on clay tablets. These texts provide insights into the Hittite society, its values, and its approach to justice.

  • Codified Laws: The Hittites had a codified system of laws, known as the "Hittite Laws" or the "Code of the Nesilim." This code was likely compiled during the reign of King Telipinu (around 1500 BC) and served as a basis for the legal system.
  • Punishment and Restitution: The Hittite laws prescribed various punishments for crimes, including fines, corporal punishment, and even the death penalty for serious offenses. However, the laws also allowed for restitution, where the offender could compensate the victim or their family for the harm caused.
  • Family and Inheritance Laws: The Hittite laws regulated family matters, such as marriage, divorce, and inheritance. They recognized the rights of both men and women in matters of property and inheritance, although men generally had more privileges.
  • Slavery and Labor Laws: The Hittite laws addressed the status and treatment of slaves, as well as the rights and obligations of free laborers and artisans.
  • Commercial and Contract Laws: The Hittites had laws governing commercial transactions, contracts, and property rights, reflecting their involvement in trade and economic activities.
  • Education and Religion Laws: The Hittite laws regulated education and religion, and the rights and obligations of the Hittite religious community.
  • Judicial System: The Hittite legal system had a hierarchical structure, with local courts and a higher court presided over by the king or his representatives. The king was considered the ultimate authority in matters of law and justice.
  • Influence on Other Cultures: The Hittite legal system influenced the legal traditions of neighboring cultures, such as the Hurrians and the Mesopotamians, and may have even had an impact on the later development of Greek and Roman law.

The Hittite laws provide valuable insights into the social, economic, and cultural aspects of this ancient civilization. They demonstrate the Hittites' efforts to establish a structured legal system and maintain order within their society.
