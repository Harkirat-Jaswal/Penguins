Economy

The ancient Anatolian civilization's rich economy


The Hittite Empire had a prosperous economy based on agriculture, trade, and skilled craftsmanship. The fertile lands along the major rivers, such as the Halys, Euphrates, and Tigris, were crucial for the empire's food production. The Hittites grew crops like wheat, barley, and grapes, and raised livestock such as cattle, sheep, and goats.

Trade played a significant role in the Hittite economy. The empire controlled important trade routes that connected Anatolia with Mesopotamia, Syria, and the Mediterranean region, facilitating the exchange of goods and cultural influences. The Hittites traded in various commodities, including textiles, pottery, metalwork, and luxury items.

Skilled craftsmen in the Hittite Empire produced high-quality goods, such as bronze and iron tools, weapons, jewelry, and pottery. The Hittites were known for their advanced metalworking techniques, and their iron products were highly valued in the ancient world.

The Hittite economy was also supported by a system of taxation and tribute from conquered territories. The empire collected taxes and tributes in the form of goods, labor, and precious materials, which contributed to its wealth and power.
